The importance of spelling


Many people doubt the importance of editing; in this day and age of spell-checkers and online dictionaries, it seems unnecessary to pay for a service your computer can do for free. This is a common misconception, which can be demonstrated by the following poem.


Eye have a spelling checker;

It came with my PC.

It plane lee marks four my revue

Mistakes I cannot sea.

I’ve run this poem threw it,

I’m sure your pleas to no,

Its letter perfect in it’s weigh

My chequer tolled me sew.


Common spell-checkers will not pick up correctly spelled words when they are used in the wrong context. Editors are trained to find these incongruen-cies, but their job involves much more than just spell-checking. Editors make documents easier to read, by ensuring correct spelling, punctuation, gram-mar, format and consistency, and they can also restructure documents, paragraphs and sentences so that the document flows better. The easier the document is to understand, the less time people need to spend reading it, which means they spend their time productively. Did you know


Did you know that, with Tenders Online, you can generate a report of ten-ders that are likely to be released in the future, months or even years in advance? Using the “Tender Contract End Date” search feature, you can find a list of contracts that are due to finish within a certain time period (and would therefore be likely to be retendered prior to these dates).


Tenders Online is currently showing a total of 1517 contracts due to expire in the 6 months commencing 01/05/15. These encompass tenders going back as far as 2005 right up to tenders released this month. So even if you’re across all of the recent tenders in your industry, unless you have been tendering in your industry for 10 years this search will provide you with valuable insight and ability to forecast upcoming contracts.
For example, the following tender is from 2005, and was a 10 year contract due to finish on 28/12/15.
