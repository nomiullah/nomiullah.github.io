How Do We Rank

Unbiased. Community Driven. Transparent.

CrowdReviews.com rates each product or service along 5 parameters for each category, depending on the products or services they offer. These parameters are further made up of independently-verifiable, transparent factors that make our ranking algorithm truly unique. Some of the factors that go into making the model are detailed below.

Review Strength

Number of Reviews

The algorithm takes into consideration the total number of reviews.

Example: A credit card processing company with lots of reviews will fare better than a credit card processing company that has fewer reviews.

Category Specific Reviews

The total number of reviews tied to a specific category are taken into consideration by the algorithm.

Example: A web design company with lots of web design client reviews will fare better than a web design company that has a lot of Drupal development client reviews.

Average Age of Reviews

The average age of the review is a factor used by the algorithm to determine rankings.

Example: A marketing automation software company with reviews that average three months old will outperform a marketing automation software company with reviews that average four months old.

Percentage of Anonymous Reviews

Anonymous reviews are given less weightage when compared to reviews where the reviewer has opted to have their identity published next to the review.

Example: An HR software company with reviews associated with identities will rank better than a HR software company with anonymous reviews.

Average Rating

Our rankings take into consideration the average rating based on the five-star rating a reviewer gives a product or service.

Example: A payroll company provider with an average star rating of 4 will rank better than a payroll company with an average star rating of 3.

Reputation Strength

Number of Negative Reviews

Negative reviews have an inverse relationship with a product's or service's position within the rankings.

Example: A job costing software company with a higher percentage of negative reviews will rank lower than a job costing software company with a lower percentage of negative reviews.

Percentage of Negative Reviews Resolved

We offer vendors the opportunity to address and resolve negative reviews as a means of allowing them to handle a situation.

Example: A rehab center that resolves issues will rank higher than one that doesn't.

Reviewer Strength

Client Reviews

Client reviews are given the second highest consideration due to their direct use of the products and services.

Example: A job costing software company with a higher percentage of negative reviews will rank lower than a job costing software company with a lower percentage of negative reviews.

Competitor Reviews

Positive reviews left by competitors are given the highest level of consideration as they are commending their own competitors.

Example: Presuming they are equally rated, a responsive web design company that receives 3 positive reviews from its competitors will fare better than one with 3 positive reviews from its clients.

Employee Reviews

Employee reviews are also given consideration due to their internal involvement in producing products and services.

Example: Presuming they are equally rated, an education software company with 5 employee reviews will fare better than an education software company with 5 potential client reviews.

Percentage of LinkedIn Reviews

A LinkedIn reviewer is generally considered to have provided an authentic review therefore significant consideration is provided to such reviews.

Example: Presuming they are equally rated, a legal software company with positive LinkedIn reviews will outperform a legal software company with no LinkedIn reviews.

Potential Client Reviews

The algorithm also takes into consideration the opinions of potential customers which have not bought for one reason or another.

Example: Presuming they are equally rated, a company with 5 potential client reviews will fare better than a company with 5 reviews with no classification attached.

Other Reviews

The least amount of weight is placed on reviews left by non-disclosed parties.

Example: Presuming they are equally rated, an iPhone developer with 5 reviews with no classification attached will fare worse than an iPhone Developer with 5 reviews that disclose the type of reviewer.

Profile Strength

Profile Completeness

We take the completion of profiles very seriously, therefore the overall completeness of a profile is taken into consideration by the algorithm.

Other Reviews

Example: Presuming they are equally rated, an iPhone developer with 5 reviews with no classification attached will fare worse than an iPhone Developer with 5 reviews that disclose the type of reviewer.

Minimum Number of Reviews

The algorithm gives higher weightage to companies that have a minimum of 5 reviews.

Minimum Number of LinkedIn Reviews

The algorithm gives higher weightage to companies that have a minimum of 3 LinkedIn reviews.

Profile Claimed

The algorithm takes into consideration whether a profile has been claimed by a representative or not.

Verified Status

CrowdReviews.com wants to make sure the data presented to its visitors is accurate. We give a substantial advantage to those companies that are willing to be transparent and honest about their credentials.

Letter From CPA, CFP, CFA, CMA or an Attorney

In order for company key facts to be verified, we request companies to submit the Company Key Facts Certification Form.

Simply fill out the form and have a certified public accountant, chartered financial analyst, certified management accountant, or attorney sign the form attesting to the complete accuracy of the information. The certifier may work at the applicant firm or be independent, as long as the certifier is a licensed professional with credentials that are current and in good standing.
